## Bottom Line

The article is a standard financial news report on Palantir's quarterly earnings, focusing on revenue growth, stock price increases, and general AI momentum. While it mentions Palantir's significant growth in government sales, it does not engage with the theoretical mechanisms of the state's constitutive role in building tech dominance (C3) or the distinction between capitalist profit and feudal rent (C4). It reports outcomes—such as a $1 billion revenue quarter—and does not analyze the mechanisms of surplus extraction or the structural relationship between the state and capital. Consequently, it fails multiple relevance gates (G2, G5) and is symmetrically compatible with any political-economy framework, providing no discriminating evidence for the theory map.
