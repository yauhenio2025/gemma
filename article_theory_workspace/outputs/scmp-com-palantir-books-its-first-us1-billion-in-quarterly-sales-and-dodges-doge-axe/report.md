## Bottom Line

The article provides a concrete, though narrow, illustrative case of the symbiotic relationship between a high-tech AI firm and the state, supporting the theory's claim that state power is not 'parcelized' but selectively directed (C3). The fact that Palantir's government revenue grew by 53% while the state implemented broad austerity measures via the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) suggests a strategic alignment where the state prioritizes specific technological capabilities over general spending. However, the article is marginal because it focuses on a single firm's financial outcomes rather than providing structural data on capital expenditure or a systemic analysis of rent versus profit.
