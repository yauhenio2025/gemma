## Bottom Line

The article is a standard news report on China's industrial policy, focusing on GDP growth projections and the expansion of computing infrastructure. It fails all five relevance gates. While it mentions state investment and US-China competition, it does so at the level of macroeconomic 'growth engines' and geopolitical rivalry, rather than engaging with the specific theoretical mechanisms of the techno-feudalism debate. It provides no analysis of rent versus profit, the nature of surplus extraction, or the constitutive role of the state in the mode of production as defined in the theory map.
