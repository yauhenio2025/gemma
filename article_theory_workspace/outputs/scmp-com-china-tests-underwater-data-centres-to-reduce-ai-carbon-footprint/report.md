## Bottom Line

The article describes a specific project by a Chinese firm, Highlander, to deploy underwater data centers for AI computing. While it is a narrow case study, it provides illustrative evidence for two core claims: C1 (tech firms as productive capitalists) by demonstrating the high physical capital intensity and engineering complexity required for AI infrastructure, and C3 (the state's constitutive role) by detailing government subsidies and partnerships with state-owned construction companies. It does not provide the systematic data needed to adjudicate the rent-vs-profit debate or the structural nature of tech dominance, but it grounds the 'cloud' in material, energy-intensive production.

## Article Snapshot

- Summary: A Chinese company, Highlander, is deploying an underwater data center pod in Shanghai to reduce the energy consumption and carbon footprint of AI computing, leveraging ocean currents for cooling. The project is part of a broader government push for greener data centers, supported by government subsidies. The project faces technical challenges (corrosion, connectivity, and security) and environmental concerns regarding thermal pollution of marine ecosystems.
- Main claims: Underwater data centers can reduce cooling energy consumption by approximately 90%., The Shanghai project is one of the world's first commercial services of its kind., Underwater facilities can complement, rather than replace, traditional land-based data centers.

- Theory thesis: The 'techno-feudalism' thesis—that digital platforms have replaced capitalist profit-making with feudal rent extraction—reflects analytical weakness on the left rather than genuine systemic transformation. Tech giants are better understood as capitalist producers making massive investments in production, not as parasitic feudal rentiers. The thesis arises from unresolved tensions in Marxist debates (Brenner vs. Wallerstein) about exploitation versus expropriation, and from frameworks ('user-ism,' cognitive capitalism) that misidentify the sources of tech-company revenues. A more expansive conception of capitalism that integrates both exploitation and expropriation eliminates the need to invoke feudalism.
