## Bottom Line

The article describes China's use of the 'unreliable entity list' and rare earth export controls to sanction US defense and semiconductor intelligence firms. This provides a concrete, illustrative example of state power actively shaping the tech landscape and controlling market access for geopolitical leverage. This directly maps to the theory's focus on the state's constitutive role in tech dominance (C3) and the US-China tech rivalry (T4), contradicting the techno-feudalist claim that sovereignty has been 'parcelized' or superseded by platform power. However, as a single news report on a specific set of sanctions, it serves as a data point rather than a structural proof of the state's overall role in the global economy.

## Article Snapshot

- Summary: China's Ministry of Commerce has added 14 Western entities, primarily US-based defense and intelligence firms, to its 'unreliable entity list,' banning them from trade and investment in China. This action, which includes a semiconductor data analysis firm (TechInsights), coincided with new export controls on rare earth materials, occurring shortly before a scheduled meeting between Presidents Xi Jinping and Donald Trump.
- Main claims: Beijing is using sanctions and export controls as leverage for upcoming diplomatic meetings with the US., The sanctioned entities are accused of military-technical cooperation with Taiwan and assisting foreign governments in suppressing Chinese companies., The Chinese government maintains that it still welcomes law-abiding foreign investment.
